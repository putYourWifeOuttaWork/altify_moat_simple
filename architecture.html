Today's architecture · Shipped for two decades

Under the coach, under the loop, there is a machine. It runs on a structure we already own.

Enterprise selling runs as three layers: sellers execute on top, humans and AI collaborate in the middle, and underneath sits the architecture layer. This page starts with the part that already exists: the data architecture Altify has shipped for twenty years, a structural replica of a deal's interior. First we teach the structure. Then we show the machine that brings it to life.

Today's architecture · One deal, used everywhere

Every drawing on this page uses the same deal, so you can watch one thing get understood, not many things get gestured at. One renewal and expansion opportunity. Nine stakeholders. Fourteen calls, about sixty emails, two quarters of history.

Today's architecture · Who

Contacts become a political structure.

A CRM stores names. A people map stores the politics: who reports to whom, who influences whom, who is with us, who is against us, and who actually matters.

Today's architecture · What they care about

What each person actually cares about, written down.

Problems, priorities, and pressures, each linked to the people who hold them. Not notes about the deal: structure inside it.

Today's architecture · The current state

People become relationships. Insights become problems. Together they are the deal's political state.

Neither map is the point. The point is that they reference each other, so the deal has one connected picture of who matters and why, right now.

Today's architecture · The questions

The questions a deal must answer, graded against the maps.

An assessment is a fixed set of questions every deal of this type must answer: is the economic buyer engaged, is the pain quantified, do we have a coach. The answers are not opinions typed in a box. They are read off the maps.

Today's architecture · Assess, then address

First the grade. Then the to-do.

Motion one, assess: the maps answer the questions and status appears, including red flags, conditions that must be true and aren't. Motion two, address: every gap becomes a required action. The grade tells you what to do: the strategic view of the future state, and the tactical view of what's red today.

Today's architecture · Which rules apply

The deal's own attributes choose its questions.

Which methodology, which questions, which process: selected by rule from solution, segment, and motion. Fifty divisions can run fifty calibrated question sets in one org.

Today's architecture · Memory

One record per person, with dated nodes beneath it.

When something important changes, a node is written under the record: role changed, sentiment shifted, flag raised, each with a date. The record deepens. It never multiplies.

Today's architecture · The summary

The questions. The situation. The history.

That is the whole structure, and it is the shape of a great deal review: hold the standard, the situation, and the history apart, so each stays true. One more thing is true of it: only a deliberate write ever changes anything. Everything else is a read.

Signals resolve by narrowing, not by matching.

A call or email arrives. The seller's own address bounds it to their five open deals: zero inference. Calendar attendees key most voices to known people: zero inference. What remains is a pick from a short list, the deal's nine mapped people, where models are strong and hallucination has nowhere to land.1 The truly ambiguous residue routes to a human with a citation. People mentioned but unknown are never merged by probability: the system proposes a new stakeholder, a human ratifies, and that identity is settled once, forever.

Keys first. Lists second. People only for the residue.

Yellow means drafted, not true yet.

Every AI-drafted value carries its citation and a confidence level. High confidence writes with full audit. Low confidence waits as a yellow flag until a human ratifies it. Nothing yellow is ever treated as truth, rolled up, or graded. Complete, not yet true, until someone says so.

Every ratified change writes a node. Automatically. Forever.

The moment a change passes the gate, a node is written: what changed, its source, its citation, who ratified it, when. Each entity keeps one card of current truth and the journal of nodes beneath it. A rule decides which value wins when they conflict.2 Old nodes compact into period summaries on a cadence. Nothing is re-derived later; the meaning was written the moment it mattered.

An activation, not a build.

Ratified records stream to the platform's native data engine inside the customer's tenant over the free connector. Consolidation is a key join, because every record already owns an ID: the platform's expensive probabilistic unification engine is never enabled. Nightly pattern math runs as scheduled batch computation, priced in fractions of a cent per million rows,11 inside an allowance most enterprise customers already hold.12

The rulebook amends. The playbook advises.

Two loops read the ratified record, in a required order. The analysis loop mines what actually killed deals and, past a threshold, proposes an amendment: a new condition that must be true. Humans ratify; a version ships. The optimization loop watches what the best sellers do inside the rules and coaches everyone else, continuously, as advice. Methodology first: you cannot compute better against an undefined standard. Both loops learn only from ratified truth, which is why they compound instead of drift.

The AI architecture · What this layer delivers upward

Determinism is not a pillar. It is the product.

Ask twice, get the same answer twice, and get told what's missing. In one measurement, a frontier model asked the same question a thousand times at its most careful setting gave eighty different answers.3 This layer gives one. Everything above stands on it: the daily brief, the Monday agenda, the monthly patterns, all detailed on the collaboration layer page.

The comparison · What the job demands

Five demands before any comparison is fair.

Enterprise selling is unlike most AI work. A support bot can be ninety percent right across a million tickets, because errors average out. A quarter has a dozen deals that matter, so errors concentrate. That difference writes the criteria.

The comparison · Their best machine

The 2026 standard, at our fixture.

Take the same deal: nine stakeholders, fourteen calls, sixty emails. The modern standard captures all of it, extracts entities and meaning with models, probabilistically resolves them to people and deals, keeps a persistent memory in the vendor's cloud, an extracted graph with summarized clusters or a series of snapshots, then retrieves and synthesizes an answer per question, and writes updates back.9 The newest variants defer graph building to query time entirely. Every serious system now answers anywhere; access is table stakes, and the contest is what the answers stand on.

The comparison · The honest concession

For most of the market, this is the right machine.

Short cycles, few stakeholders, high volume: small business, consumer, transactional selling, support. There, errors average out, nobody governs a methodology, and this design's gifts are decisive: zero setup, value on day one, and it hears everything, including the unknown-unknowns no schema anticipated, the competitor mentioned once in call nine.9

THE STACK, TECHNICALLY

Closed, expert-defined schema. The declared structure is the customer's own methodology encoded as objects, fields, and typed relationships: relationship maps, insight maps, process steps, assessments, all children of one opportunity ID with deterministic foreign keys. Nothing about the structure is model-generated. The structure is a graph in the strict sense: typed nodes, typed edges, one root. It is declared, versioned, and admin-extensible; instances are dynamic, the schema changes only by amendment.

Data as metadata. Which methodology applies, which questions it asks, and which process governs are selected by rule from the deal's own attributes: solution, segment, motion. Fifty divisions can run fifty calibrated question sets in one tenant. The structure is dynamic without being inferred.

The write edge. Extraction runs once per new signal, at ingestion, behind a resolution funnel: deterministic keys narrow first (the seller's address bounds the deal set, calendar attendees key most voices), closed-set selection assigns the rest (the model picks from the deal's mapped people, a list, not a world), and the truly ambiguous residue routes to a human with a citation. People mentioned but unknown are never probabilistically merged: the system proposes a new stakeholder, a human ratifies, and the identity question is answered once, forever. Every extracted value carries a citation and a confidence level. Low confidence routes to ratification; high confidence writes with full audit. Inference exists here, quarantined, and caught.

The read edge. Answers are assembled by walking the structure and computing over it. Model inference at read time is limited to sense-making over already-true values: narrating why a computed flag exists, assembling the morning brief. A fraction of the volume, and never the source of facts.

Time, in two grains. Each entity keeps one profile card of current truth, upserted, and one append-only journal of ratified nodes beneath it, each node carrying source, citation, ratifier, and timestamp. Deterministic survivorship decides which value wins. Compaction rolls old nodes into period summaries on a cadence, so the record deepens without hoarding. Consolidation across an entity's many per-deal cards is a key join plus a materialized entity view: the platform's profile-unification engine is never enabled, because deterministic foreign keys make it unnecessary, and its cost, orders of magnitude above batch computation, is never incurred.

The memory layer, concretely. Ratified records stream to the platform's native data engine inside the customer's tenant over the free CRM connector. Pattern analysis runs as scheduled batch calculated insights, priced per million rows scanned11; threshold conditions fire native triggered flows that create recommendation records, post to chat, or feed an agent. Weekly and monthly cadences keep consumption inside the free allowance most enterprise editions already include12. Streaming computation and profile unification are excluded from the design on purpose. Generative narration, where used, runs through the platform's governed AI gateway; the batch layer is tenant-local by architecture and never leaves.

The delivery edge. The brief subsystem is one-way publication of rendered content, never record sync: a preference registry (asked at design time: where does this team work), a per-timezone assembler that runs after the nightly write completes, channel adapters (email and chat natively, other suites via governed webhook), and a ledger logging every brief sent: content, time, door. Agents pull the same brief over the open assistant protocol instead of being pushed. Ratification from any door returns through the governed loop.

Packaging. Platform rules require the memory-layer metadata to ship as its own package beside the existing application package, distributed as a data kit; the pattern is the same one the platform vendor uses for its own industry kits. The core loop requires no custom code beyond the assembler, the adapters, and the write edge.

WHY EACH CHOICE, THE EVIDENCE

Why a closed schema: models cannot reliably detect what is absent from open text: frontier models reach only about 70% F1 finding deliberate omissions even at small context sizes, because attention cannot attend to gaps.1 A closed schema turns absence into a null-field lookup. Every red flag is exactly that. And in this vertical, absence detection is the job: qualification is the discipline of finding what's missing.

Why a normative schema: a structure extracted from conversations can only describe what was said. A structure declared by methodology prescribes what must be true, which is the only way a machine can compute a red flag, and the only way it can compute a contention: the present disagreeing with the ratified past. Descriptive stores cannot raise either, because they contain no obligations and no ratified history to contend with.

Why normativity cannot be extracted: transcripts record events; obligations are not events. A condition that must be true is authored by experts, never spoken by customers, so no extraction pipeline can recover it at any scale.

Why fleet questions need a shared scale: roll-ups across hundreds of deals require every deal to answer the same question on the same scale. Declared properties guarantee it; emergent properties (sentiment, temperature, enthusiasm as synonyms) cannot be compared, survived, or rolled up, so portfolio absence questions become unanswerable in principle.

Why the standard is right below the line: high-velocity, low-stakeholder selling gets its value from zero-setup capture and discovery; error averaging makes probabilistic answers acceptable; methodology governance is absent, so normativity has nothing to bind to. The concession is genuine.

Why computation over judgment: replacing model judgment with a deterministic rule improved accuracy by 10.8 points, widening to 21 at long context, while a leading temporal knowledge graph scored 7% on the same conflict-resolution task.2 Our survivorship, scoring, and escalation are those rules. Severity is computed from role, transition, and dwell time, never declared by a model.

Why not generation-time answers: identical temperature-zero sampling produced 80 distinct outputs in 1,000 runs.3 Long context degrades 30 to 50% before advertised limits, worst on cross-referencing.5 Retrieving pre-computed state uses about 90% fewer tokens than re-reading history.4

Why ratified citations: roughly half of citations generated at answer time are invalid on audit.6 Ours are written at ingestion and human-ratified where uncertain, following the proven pattern of routing the uncertain few percent to people.7

Why closed-set resolution: open-set identification, matching a name against the world, is where probabilistic systems spend fortunes and still err; the field's patent portfolios are monuments to it. Closed-set selection, picking one of nine mapped people, is where extraction models are strong and hallucination has nowhere to land. The schema converts the hard problem into the easy one before any model runs.

Why authored time: snapshots preserve state and discard meaning; recovering "what changed and why" from frames requires re-inference on every read, forever. Writing the node once, at ratification, with its citation and its ratifier, makes history cheap to carry and free to cite. Fixed lookback windows in interaction-corpus systems are the tell of the opposite cost model: when every question re-reads the past, the past must be capped.

Why per-tenant learning: the rulebook loop calibrates each customer's declared structure from their own closed-lost history, at design time, and amendments ship as versions after human ratification. Nothing is trained across customers, and nothing learned leaves the tenant. Cross-tenant learning builds the vendor's asset; per-tenant learning builds the customer's.

The honest graph comparison: emergent graph retrieval is real progress and wins real jobs: multi-hop discovery and thematic summarization over unknown corpora are its documented strengths.9 The same benchmark literature shows it underperforming plain retrieval on simple factual and time-sensitive questions at multiplied latency,10 and the field's own consensus now pairs graphs with deterministic computation in front of structured data. That hybrid conclusion is this page's design, stated from the other direction.

The shared weakness, said plainly: our write edge depends on the same extraction reliability that limits automatic knowledge graphs, with published error rates from a few percent to roughly a quarter in open graphs.8 Confidence routing, closed-set narrowing, and ratification contain it. Nothing eliminates it. The highest-value numbers we can publish are our own measured extraction precision and ratification catch rate, and the protocol for both is defined with a date.
